COVID-19 Deaths in the United States

By Alexandra Tamvakis
November 2, 2020

Since its outbreak in early 2020, the COVID-19 virus has wreaked havoc upon the nation. With the death toll in the United States surpassing 200,000 as of November 2020, let's break down the lives lost as a result of the coronavirus from May to August 2020.

There has been much discussion in the media about the elevated risk that senior Americans face in regards to the coronavirus. This is starkly visible when analyzing the number of deaths as a result of COVID-19 by age. As the following chart shows, the vast majority of deaths have occurred to people 65 years old or older.

Different regions of the country have fared differently as well. The increased number of cases in the South may be a result of differing reactions to the coronavirus--many larger metropolitan areas (such as those in the Northeast or Western U.S.) established strict lockdown procedures earlier in the year.
